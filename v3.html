🛠️ How did you make your first prototype?

I created my first prototype by drawing the shape of my design on cardboard and cutting out the layers. I thought about who my target users would be and why they would like my design. 🎯

🔪 What cutting tips are the most important to you?

I learned to use a coping saw to cut MDF pieces safely. The most important tips for me are to secure the piece properly, cut at the correct angle, and take my time for accuracy. 🪓

📏 How did you cut out the layers? What was easy or difficult about it?

To cut the layers, I started with the middle layer and made careful cuts for the pencil space. Cutting out small shapes was challenging, but sanding made it easier to get a good fit. ✂️🖊️

🧩 How did you assemble the layers?

I assembled the layers by carefully gluing them together, making sure everything lined up. I chose to tape some pieces first before gluing to avoid cracking the thin layers. 🧷👏

❓ How did you get a smooth finish?

I used a hand file, then sandpaper, and finally wet and dry paper to get a smooth finish. I learned not to wet the sandpaper because wood absorbs moisture. Holding the piece properly while sanding helped me achieve a better result. 🧽💪

🔥 What have you learnt while doing the pyrography?

During the pyrography step, I used a soldering iron to burn designs into the wood. I learned to work slowly, use only one hand to hold the iron, and to be careful because it gets very hot. 🍉⚡
